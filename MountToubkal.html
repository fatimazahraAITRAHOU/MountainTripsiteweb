Description of the Trip to Mount Toubkal

Mount Toubkal is the highest peak in North Africa, rising to 4,167 meters above sea level, located in the Atlas Mountains of Morocco. The journey to the summit offers a perfect blend of challenge and adventure amidst breathtaking landscapes.During the trek, you'll pass through traditional Berber villages, lush valleys, waterfalls, and rugged mountain terrains, with snowy paths in winter. Reaching the summit rewards you with stunning panoramic views stretching over the desert and surrounding mountains.

Highlights of the Trip

  • A thrilling adventure for nature and trekking enthusiasts.
  • A unique cultural experience interacting with local Berber communities.
  • Stunning views of the sunrise and sunset from the mountains.
  • Suitable for all fitness levels with moderate physical preparation.

Itinerary (3 Days / 2 Nights)

Day 1: Arrival in Imlil

Arrival at Imlil Village (elevation 1,740 meters):

  • Depart from Marrakech in the morning and drive to Imlil (about 1.5-2 hours by car).
  • Take a short tour of the village to explore the surroundings and scenic views.
  • Enjoy a local lunch and prepare for the adventure ahead.
  • Rent or prepare trekking gear if needed.
  • Overnight stay at a traditional guesthouse in Imlil.

Day 2: Trek to Toubkal Refuge (3,207 meters)

Early Morning Start:

  • Have a hearty breakfast at the guesthouse.
  • Begin the trek to Toubkal Refuge (approximately 6-7 hours of hiking).

The Route:

  • Start by passing through the picturesque village of Armed.
  • Take a break at Sidi Chamharouch Waterfalls, a spiritual and popular rest spot.
  • Continue hiking through rocky mountain trails toward the refuge.

Arrival at the Refuge:

  • Arrive in the afternoon and rest while enjoying the stunning mountain surroundings.
  • Dinner is served at the refuge.
  • Overnight stay at the refuge or a designated tent.

Day 3: Summit Ascent and Return

Climb to Toubkal Summit (4,167 meters):

  • Wake up before dawn to begin the climb to the summit (3-4 hours).
  • Witness a mesmerizing sunrise from the peak and enjoy panoramic views.
  • Take memorable photos and savor your achievement.

Descent to Imlil:

  • Return to the refuge for breakfast.
  • Continue descending to Imlil (4-5 hours).

End of the Journey:

  • Drive back to Marrakech in the evening.
